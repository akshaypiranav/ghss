About the Project

The Drowsiness Detection System is a groundbreaking project designed to enhance road safety by detecting signs of driver fatigue in real-time. Our system uses advanced computer vision techniques to monitor eye movements and facial expressions, alerting drivers when signs of drowsiness are detected.

According to research, drowsy driving is responsible for approximately 100,000 crashes, 71,000 injuries, and 1,550 deaths annually. Our mission is to reduce these numbers by providing an accessible, open-source solution that can be implemented in any vehicle.

How It Works

1
Face Detection

The system uses Haar Cascade classifiers to detect and locate the face in the video stream.

2
Eye Region Extraction

Once the face is detected, the region containing the eyes is extracted for further analysis.

3
Eye State Analysis

Using dlib's facial landmarks, we track eye movements and measure the Eye Aspect Ratio (EAR).

4
Drowsiness Detection

The system analyzes blink patterns and eye closure duration to determine if the driver is drowsy.

5
Alert Generation

If drowsiness is detected, the system triggers visual and audio alerts to wake the driver.
